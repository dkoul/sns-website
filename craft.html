The Art of Sozni & Kashida

Centuries-old embroidery techniques that transform fabric into heirloom art.

Sozni Embroidery

Sozni is a traditional form of Kashmiri needlework known for its intricate patterns and delicate stitching. The name comes from the Persian word for needle—"sozun"—and the craft has been practiced in Kashmir for centuries.

Master artisans use fine silk or cotton threads to create elaborate designs on pashmina shawls, stoles, and other textiles. Each piece can take 6-12 months to complete, with some masterworks requiring even longer.

Kashida Work

Kashida is another ancient embroidery technique characterized by its bold, flowing patterns and use of vibrant silk threads. Unlike Sozni's delicate precision, Kashida features larger, more dramatic motifs.

This technique has been used to adorn royal garments and ceremonial textiles for generations, and today remains a symbol of luxury and cultural heritage.

The Process

Creating a single piece involves numerous steps, each requiring years of training to master.

1. Design

Patterns are hand-drawn or traced from traditional motifs, often inspired by nature and Persian art.

2. Embroidery

Master artisans stitch each thread by hand, working for months to complete intricate patterns.

3. Finishing

Each piece is meticulously inspected and finished, ensuring it meets the highest standards of quality.
